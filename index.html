Who do I teach?

Complete Beginners

Never touched a piano? That's fine. Lessons start with the basics and quickly build into real music, so students feel achievement early and often.

Children

A mix of piano playing and short book-based activities that build reading and theory without losing momentum. Changing focus at the right moments keeps lessons lively and gives every child plenty of chances to succeed.

Exam Students

For students aiming for formal results, lessons can be shaped around international music exams with a clear roadmap, technique work, theory knowledge, and exam-style practice.

Returning Adults

Lessons can be relaxed and practical - focusing on the music you want to play while building reading and technique in a straightforward way.

Common Questions

How much do lessons cost?

Lessons are $90 for 60 minutes. Each session is a full hour - enough time for technique, piece work, theory, and a clear homework plan.

How long are lessons?

Lessons are one hour. This gives enough time for a proper warm-up, piece work, theory, and a clear homework plan without rushing.

Do you teach complete beginners?

Yes. Beginners are very welcome - no prior experience required. Lessons start from the very beginning and progress at a comfortable pace.

Do you set homework?

Yes. Students receive clear weekly homework so progress continues between lessons. It's designed to be realistic and specific - not overwhelming.

Can you prepare students for exams?

Yes. Preparation for international music exams is available, including supporting theory, technique, and exam-style practice.

Can parents wait during lessons?

Yes. Parents and siblings are always welcome to wait. You're invited into a home, not a waiting room.

Is there a dog?

There is a friendly little dog - a favourite with many children. The dog can be kept away during lessons if preferred.

Where are lessons held?

In a lovely calm home studio in Gulf Harbour, Whangaparaoa, with a beautiful sea view. A welcoming, relaxed environment for learning.
